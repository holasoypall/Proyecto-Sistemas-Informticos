Where does EFI come from?

EFI (Extensible Firmware Interface) is an interface between the system hardware and the operating system that was developed by Intel as a replacement for the older BIOS (Basic Input/Output System). One of its main purposes is to provide a more flexible and modern environment for system booting and hardware management.


What does the EFI do?


Its main functions include:

  • Initialize and test the hardware components when you turn on the computer.

  • Load the operating system (Windows or Linux for example).

  • It allows more advanced configurations than the old BIOS, supports large hard drives like GPT instead of MBR, and has a more modern and visual interface.


Some of its main features are:

  • Its file format is FAT32 and its size ranges between 100 and 500MB.

  • Its partition type is EFI System Partition in GPT and its location varies, but it is usually found at the beginning of the disk.
